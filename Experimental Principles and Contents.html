Experimental principles and contents

A three-way catalytic converter is fitted to a car’s exhaust system to convert harmful emissions like carbon monoxide, hydrocarbons, and nitrogen oxides into harmless carbon dioxide, water, and nitrogen. The converter’s catalyst prompts oxidation-reduction reactions at high temperatures, effectively purifying the exhaust gases.

The use of a three-way catalyst cleaner is meant to improve the functioning of the car's internal three-way catalytic components. Furthermore, the variation in pollutants present in the tail gas before and after the cleaning process is measured to evaluate the effectiveness of the purification.
