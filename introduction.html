Introduction

China's economic growth has heightened focus on life quality, especially lin health and environmental sustainability. A key concern is the impact of vehicle exhaust , a significant source of air pollution, on public health . Vehicle exhaust contains various harmful substances, including carbon monoxide (CO), carbon dioxide (CO₂), nitrogen oxides (NOx), particulate matter (PM), and hydrocarbons (HC). Each of these poses distinct risks to health and the environment. Carbon Monoxide (CO), a colorless, odorless gas, disrupts blood's oxygen transport, potentially leading to fatal outcomes. Nitrogen Dioxide (NO₂) harms the respiratory system and contributes to air pollution through smog and particulate formation. Particulate Matter (PM), consisting of fine and inhalable particles, can cause serious respiratory and cardiovascular conditions. Hydrocarbons (HC) from unburned fuel contribute to photochemical smog, with some being carcinogenic. The collective effect of these pollutants from vehicle exhaust is not only a worsening of air quality but also an increase in health risks, such as respiratory and heart diseases, and a contribution to global warming. Addressing this issue is therefore crucial for improving public health and the urban environment in China.

Efforts to mitigate vehicle exhaust emissions include advancing vehicle technology and enforcing stricter emission standards . These measures are vital for balancing China's continued economic development with the need for environmental protection. Effectively managing vehicle exhaust pollution is key to ensuring a healthier , more sustainable future for China's urban areas and its inhabitants.
